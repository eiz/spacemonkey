Q: What is public policy?
A: Public policy is a course of action or inaction chosen by public authorities to address a particular problem or issue.

Q: Who makes public policy?
A: Public policy is made by elected officials, government agencies, and other stakeholders.

Q: What are the different types of public policy?
A: The different types of public policy include regulatory policy, distributive policy, redistributive policy, and constituent policy.

Q: What is regulatory policy?
A: Regulatory policy is a type of public policy that seeks to regulate the behavior of individuals and organizations.

Q: What is distributive policy?
A: Distributive policy is a type of public policy that seeks to distribute resources or benefits to a particular group or region.

Q: What is redistributive policy?
A: Redistributive policy is a type of public policy that seeks to redistribute resources or benefits from one group to another.

Q: What is constituent policy?
A: Constituent policy is a type of public policy that seeks to address the needs and concerns of a particular group of constituents.

Q: What is the policy-making process?
A: The policy-making process is the process by which public policy is formulated, adopted, and implemented.

Q: What are the stages of the policy-making process?
A: The stages of the policy-making process include agenda setting, policy formulation, adoption, implementation, and evaluation.

Q: What is agenda setting?
A: Agenda setting is the process by which issues are identified and placed on the policy agenda.

Q: What is policy formulation?
A: Policy formulation is the process by which policy options are developed and evaluated.

Q: What is policy adoption?
A: Policy adoption is the process by which a policy is approved by the relevant decision-making body.

Q: What is policy implementation?
A: Policy implementation is the process by which a policy is put into practice.

Q: What is policy evaluation?
A: Policy evaluation is the process by which the effectiveness of a policy is assessed.

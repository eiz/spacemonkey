Q: What is developmental psychology?
A: Developmental psychology is the scientific study of how and why human beings change over the course of their life.

Q: What are the three domains of development?
A: The three domains of development are physical, cognitive, and socioemotional.

Q: What is the difference between maturation and learning?
A: Maturation refers to the biological unfolding of development, while learning refers to the acquisition of knowledge or skills through experience.

Q: What is the nature vs. nurture debate?
A: The nature vs. nurture debate is a longstanding argument about the relative importance of genetic and environmental factors in shaping human development.

Q: What is the critical period hypothesis?
A: The critical period hypothesis suggests that there are specific periods of time during development when certain experiences are necessary for normal development to occur.

Q: What is the difference between a sensitive period and a critical period?
A: A sensitive period is a time when an organism is particularly responsive to certain environmental stimuli, while a critical period is a time when a particular experience is necessary for normal development to occur.

Q: What is the difference between longitudinal and cross-sectional research designs?
A: Longitudinal research designs follow the same group of individuals over a period of time, while cross-sectional research designs compare different groups of individuals at the same point in time.

Q: What is the difference between a cohort effect and a time-of-measurement effect?
A: A cohort effect is a difference between groups of individuals that is due to the time period in which they were born, while a time-of-measurement effect is a difference between groups of individuals that is due to the time period in which they were assessed.

Q: What is the difference between a genotype and a phenotype?
A: A genotype refers to an individual's genetic makeup, while a phenotype refers to an individual's observable characteristics.

Q: What is the difference between a dominant and a recessive gene?
A: A dominant gene is one that is expressed when paired with a recessive gene, while a recessive gene is only expressed when paired with another recessive gene.

Q: What is the difference between a gene and an allele?
A: A gene is a segment of DNA that codes for a particular trait, while an allele is a specific version of a gene.

Q: What is the difference between monozygotic and dizygotic twins?
A: Monozygotic twins, or identical twins, develop from a single fertilized egg and share 100% of their genes, while dizygotic twins, or fraternal twins, develop from two separate fertilized eggs and share 50% of their genes on average.

Q: What is the difference between heritability and heredity?
A: Heritability refers to the proportion of variation in a trait that is due to genetic factors, while heredity refers to the passing of traits from parents to offspring.

Q: What is the difference between a teratogen and a mutation?
A: A teratogen is an environmental agent that can cause birth defects, while a mutation is a change in the DNA sequence that can be inherited or occur spontaneously.

Q: What is the difference between a reflex and a voluntary movement?
A: A reflex is an automatic response to a stimulus, while a voluntary movement is a deliberate action that is under conscious control.

Q: What is the difference between assimilation and accommodation?
A: Assimilation is the process of incorporating new information into existing schemas, while accommodation is the process of modifying existing schemas to fit new information.

Q: What is the difference between object permanence and conservation?
A: Object permanence is the understanding that objects continue to exist even when they are out of sight, while conservation is the understanding that the amount of a substance remains the same even when its appearance changes.

Q: What is the difference between egocentrism and theory of mind?
A: Egocentrism is the tendency to view the world from one's own perspective, while theory of mind is the ability to understand that other people have their own thoughts, feelings, and perspectives.

Q: What is the difference between scaffolding and zone of proximal development?
A: Scaffolding is the process of providing support and guidance to help a child learn a new skill, while zone of proximal development is the range of tasks that a child can perform with assistance but not yet independently.

Q: What is the difference between attachment and separation anxiety?
A: Attachment is the emotional bond that forms between an infant and their primary caregiver, while separation anxiety is the distress that occurs when the infant is separated from their caregiver.

Q: What is the difference between secure and insecure attachment?
A: Secure attachment is characterized by a child who is comfortable exploring their environment and seeks comfort from their caregiver when they are distressed, while insecure attachment is characterized by a child who is either avoidant or resistant to their caregiver and may show signs of distress when separated from them.

Q: What is the difference between authoritative and authoritarian parenting styles?
A: Authoritative parenting is characterized by warmth, responsiveness, and clear expectations, while authoritarian parenting is characterized by strict rules, punishment, and little warmth or responsiveness.

Q: What is the difference between gender identity and gender role?
A: Gender identity is an individual's sense of themselves as male, female, or nonbinary, while gender role is the set of behaviors, attitudes, and expectations associated with being male or female in a particular culture.

Q: What is the difference between puberty and adolescence?
A: Puberty is the period of physical development during which sexual maturity is reached, while adolescence is the period of psychological and social development that occurs between childhood and adulthood.

Q: What is the difference between identity diffusion and identity achievement?
A: Identity diffusion is the lack of a clear sense of identity, while identity achievement is the successful resolution of the identity crisis and the establishment of a clear sense of self.

Q: What is the difference between intimacy and isolation?
A: Intimacy is the ability to form close, meaningful relationships with others, while isolation is the feeling of being disconnected from others and lacking close relationships.

Q: What is the difference between generativity and stagnation?
A: Generativity is the desire to contribute to the well-being of future generations, while stagnation is the feeling of being stuck or unfulfilled in one's personal or professional life.

Q: What is the difference between ego integrity and despair?
A: Ego integrity is the feeling of satisfaction and fulfillment that comes from reflecting on one's life and accomplishments, while despair is the feeling of regret and disappointment that comes from feeling that one's life has been wasted or unfulfilled.

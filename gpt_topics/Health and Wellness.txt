Q: What is health?
A: Health is a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity.

Q: What is wellness?
A: Wellness is an active process of becoming aware of and making choices toward a healthy and fulfilling life.

Q: What are the five components of physical fitness?
A: The five components of physical fitness are cardiovascular endurance, muscular strength, muscular endurance, flexibility, and body composition.

Q: What is cardiovascular endurance?
A: Cardiovascular endurance is the ability of the heart, lungs, and blood vessels to deliver oxygen to the body's tissues during prolonged physical activity.

Q: What is muscular strength?
A: Muscular strength is the amount of force a muscle can produce in a single maximum effort.

Q: What is muscular endurance?
A: Muscular endurance is the ability of a muscle or group of muscles to sustain repeated contractions against a resistance for an extended period of time.

Q: What is flexibility?
A: Flexibility is the ability of a joint or series of joints to move through a full range of motion.

Q: What is body composition?
A: Body composition is the proportion of fat, muscle, and bone in the body.

Q: What is the recommended amount of physical activity for adults?
A: The recommended amount of physical activity for adults is at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity per week.

Q: What are some examples of moderate-intensity aerobic activity?
A: Some examples of moderate-intensity aerobic activity include brisk walking, cycling, swimming, and dancing.

Q: What are some examples of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity?
A: Some examples of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity include running, fast cycling, swimming laps, and jumping rope.

Q: What are the benefits of regular physical activity?
A: The benefits of regular physical activity include improved cardiovascular health, increased muscular strength and endurance, improved flexibility, improved body composition, and reduced risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and some cancers.

Q: What is a healthy diet?
A: A healthy diet is one that includes a variety of nutrient-dense foods from all the food groups in appropriate amounts to meet an individual's nutritional needs.

Q: What are some examples of nutrient-dense foods?
A: Some examples of nutrient-dense foods include fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and low-fat dairy products.

Q: What are some examples of foods that should be limited in a healthy diet?
A: Some examples of foods that should be limited in a healthy diet include saturated and trans fats, added sugars, and sodium.

Q: What is the recommended daily intake of fruits and vegetables?
A: The recommended daily intake of fruits and vegetables is at least 5 servings per day.

Q: What is the recommended daily intake of whole grains?
A: The recommended daily intake of whole grains is at least 3 servings per day.

Q: What is the recommended daily intake of lean proteins?
A: The recommended daily intake of lean proteins is 5-6 ounces per day.

Q: What is the recommended daily intake of low-fat dairy products?
A: The recommended daily intake of low-fat dairy products is 2-3 servings per day.

Q: What is hydration?
A: Hydration is the process of providing adequate fluids to the body to maintain proper function.

Q: How much water should an adult drink per day?
A: An adult should drink at least 8 cups (64 ounces) of water per day.

Q: What are some signs of dehydration?
A: Some signs of dehydration include thirst, dry mouth, dark urine, fatigue, and dizziness.

Q: What are some sources of hydration besides water?
A: Some sources of hydration besides water include milk, juice, sports drinks, and fruits and vegetables with high water content.

Q: What is stress?
A: Stress is the body's response to a physical or emotional demand.

Q: What are some common sources of stress?
A: Some common sources of stress include work, relationships, finances, and health problems.

Q: What are some ways to manage stress?
A: Some ways to manage stress include exercise, relaxation techniques such as deep breathing and meditation, social support, and time management.

Q: What is sleep?
A: Sleep is a natural state of rest in which the body's functions slow down and consciousness is suspended.

Q: How much sleep do adults need per night?
A: Adults need 7-9 hours of sleep per night.

Q: What are some benefits of getting enough sleep?
A: Some benefits of getting enough sleep include improved mood, better cognitive function, improved physical health, and reduced risk of chronic diseases.

Q: What are some common sleep disorders?
A: Some common sleep disorders include insomnia, sleep apnea, and restless leg syndrome.

Q: What are some ways to improve sleep quality?
A: Some ways to improve sleep quality include establishing a regular sleep schedule, creating a relaxing bedtime routine, avoiding caffeine and alcohol before bedtime, and creating a comfortable sleep environment.

Q: What is mental health?
A: Mental health is a state of well-being in which an individual can cope with the normal stresses of life, work productively, and contribute to their community.

Q: What are some common mental health disorders?
A: Some common mental health disorders include anxiety disorders, depression, bipolar disorder, and schizophrenia.

Q: What are some ways to maintain good mental health?
A: Some ways to maintain good mental health include staying physically active, getting enough sleep, eating a healthy diet, practicing relaxation techniques, and seeking professional help when needed.

Q: What is addiction?
A: Addiction is a chronic disease characterized by compulsive drug seeking and use despite harmful consequences.

Q: What are some common types of addiction?
A: Some common types of addiction include drug addiction, alcohol addiction, and gambling addiction.

Q: What are some ways to treat addiction?
A: Some ways to treat addiction include behavioral therapy, medication-assisted treatment, and support groups such as Alcoholics Anonymous.
